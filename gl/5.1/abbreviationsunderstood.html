EpiDoc Encoding

  1. <expan>co<ex>n</ex>s<ex>ul</ex></expan>
  2. <expan>Aug<ex>ustus</ex></expan>
  3. <expan> <abbr>Aug<am>g</am></abbr><ex>usti duo</ex> </expan>
  4. <expan> <abbr>pr<am><g type="stop" /></am></abbr><ex>esbyter</ex> </expan>
  5. <expan>D<ex>is</ex> M<ex>anibus</ex></expan>
  6. <expan>D<ex>is</ex></expan> <expan>M<ex>anibus</ex></expan>
  7. <expan> <abbr> <am> <g type="monogram" /> </am> </abbr> <ex>Θεοχάρης</ex> </expan>

EpiDoc appearance with standard XSLT

  1. co(n)s(ul)
  2. Aug(ustus)
  3. Aug(usti duo)
  4. pr(esbyter)
  5. D(is) M(anibus)
  6. D(is) M(anibus)
  7. (Θεοχάρης)

Notes

  • An <abbr> element within the <expan> containing the abbreviated form of the word is available but optional. It may be useful when the abbreviation contains additional markers not in the expanded form (cf. Augg and pr. above), but might require additional linking in cases where the abbreviation must be expanded by interpolation (cf. co(n)s(ul))
  • When the abbreviated form contains markers not in the expanded form, such as additional letters or punctuation, best practice is to put these in <am> (abbreviation marker) elements, which the standard stylesheets will omit when rendering the expanded form.
  • Abbreviated phrases (e.g., D(is) M(anibus)) should properly be marked-up as a group, as illustrated in the example above, but marking them as single words is not incorrect (see the second example).

Questions and issues

  • GB (2007-10-18): a problem. One example above seems to be suggesting that the <space> is an abbreviation marker. I'm not sure I understand this, and in any case it would be illegal to tag thus in P5 (from where we have borrowed the <am> element). I have eliminated the old example until we can figure out what we meant by this issue.
