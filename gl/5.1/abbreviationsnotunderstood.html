Traditional typographic representation

  • Panciera , p.17:
    1. a(- - -)
    2. Not addressed
    3. Not addressed

EpiDoc Encoding

  1. <abbr>a</abbr>
  2. <abbr>pr</abbr>
  3. <abbr>p</abbr> <abbr>r</abbr>

EpiDoc appearance with standard XSLT

  1. a(- - -)
  2. pr(- - -)
  3. p(- - -) r(- - -)

Notes

  • Examples 2. and 3. indicate the difference between a group of letters representing a single abbreviated word, and an abbreviated phrase or formula. If an abbreviation is not understood and its nature as single word or phrase cannot be determined, the procedure for uncertainty should be followed.
