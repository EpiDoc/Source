Notes

  • Gabriel Bodard (2008-04-24): it has been decided to deprecate the use of n on this element, so that <orig> without parent::<choice> means an unresolved character, as opposed to <orig> (parallel to <reg>) inside of <choice> which is a local or irregular form requiring normalization. GB: As of this change we no longer have a way to indicate the specific cases of u/v whose vocality is unresolved, or σ/ς whose context (medial/final) is unresolved. Is this a problem?
  • The general case of unresolved characters is handled by the use of the <orig> element with the n attribute containing "unresolved". For more particular cases, such as the Latin V identified by Dohnicht as ambiguously vocalic or consonantal, the attribute may be further specified with the addition of "_vocality", vel sim.
