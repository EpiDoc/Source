Alternative readings: inline encoding and display

2021-11-18

Where there are two or more possible readings or interpretations of a string of text, an editor may choose to present all options to the reader for full transparency. In EpiDoc, these readings are encoded using the app element with lem (the preferred reading; optional) and one or more rdgs (all other reading). On a project level, the editor(s) might decide to display this variety of readings in a separate apparatus criticus or in the body of the text. Here follow examples of the latter.

  • TEI definition: app ; EpiDoc-specific customization: app
  • TEI definition: lem ; EpiDoc-specific customization: lem
  • TEI definition: rdg ; EpiDoc-specific customization: rdg

<app type="alternative">
 <lem>
  <num value="13">ιγ</num>
 </lem>
 <rdg>
  <num value="13">ι<surplus>γ</surplus>
  </num>
 </rdg>
</app>
(Source)
Note: displayed in the edition as: ιγ´ resp. ι{γ}´.

A second example, with two expansions of abbreviation:

<app type="alternative">
 <lem>
  <expan>
   <abbr>χ</abbr>
   <ex>α</ex>
   <abbr>ρτελ</abbr>
   <ex>αρίῳ</ex>
  </expan>
 </lem>
 <rdg>
  <expan>
   <ex>χρυσο</ex>
   <abbr>τελ</abbr>
   <ex>ῇ</ex>
  </expan>
 </rdg>
</app>
(Source)
Note: displayed in the edition as: χ(α)ρτελ(αρίῳ) vel (χρυσο)τελ(ῇ).

The app element should have a type attribute of "alternative".
